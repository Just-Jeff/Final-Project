The Middle Ages

Hats then evolved from being just a practical tool, to that of status and wealth in Europe during the Middle Ages in 500 to 1500 AC.

Many people in the Middle Ages were serfs, meaning that they tended to the land for the upper class. Women wore bonnets and wimples made from silk or cloth, while men wore straw hats, cloth coifs and caps, and wool or felt berets.

Other hats that were worn during this period were veils and church head gear deemed acceptable by the Catholic Church. Knights of course had on helmets meant to protect themselves, and royalty wore jewels and crown meant to show their status.
